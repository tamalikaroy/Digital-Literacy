Stay Safe Online: Essential Security Habits for Students


1. Verify the URL: Always check the exact web address before entering your login details. Scammers often use slight misspellings (like univrsity.edu instead of university.edu).
2. Never pay to work or win: Legitimate internships, campus ambassador programs, or scholarships will never ask you to pay an "onboarding" or "processing" fee upfront.
3. Set transaction limits (Financial): Use your banking app to set strict daily limits on your UPI and debit card transactions so that even if compromised, the financial drain is capped.
4. Bank officials won't ask for PINs (Financial): Remember that true bank representatives or customer care agents will never ask you to share your UPI PIN, CVV, or OTP over a phone call.
5. Beware of email attachments: Do not download unexpected PDFs or ZIP files from unknown senders, as they often contain malware or keyloggers.
6. Type, don't click: Instead of clicking links in emails regarding your bank or university accounts, manually type the official website address into your browser.
7. Use a Password Manager: Stop reusing the same password for your student portal, email, and social media. Use a password manager to generate and store unique passwords.
8. Check connected devices: Regularly review the "Active Sessions" or "Connected Devices" section in your WhatsApp, Instagram, and Google accounts to ensure no unknown devices are logged in.
